Core Research Fields

Advanced Energy & Nuclear Materials

Investigating material behaviors, structural transitions, and phase stabilities under the extreme environmental configurations typical of nuclear and fusion energy applications[cite: 1]. This includes exploring specialized component parameters under high thermal thresholds and post-irradiation constraints[cite: 1].

  • Beryllium-based two-phase moderators and novel intermetallics[cite: 1]
  • Phase-pure metal carbides and high-density hydrides[cite: 1]
  • Radiation-resistant matrices for advanced reactor shielding architectures[cite: 1]

Thin-Film Engineering & Vacuum Technology

Optimizing vapor-phase deposition pathways to fabricate highly uniform, microstructurally controlled thin films for optoelectronic, smart-window, and hardware sensor operations[cite: 1]. Focused on driving processing reproducibility through structural phase tuning[cite: 1].

  • Pulsed Laser Deposition (PLD) and Magnetron Sputtering dynamics[cite: 1]
  • Phase-purity optimization of transition-metal oxides like Vanadium Dioxide (VO2)[cite: 1]
  • Bandgap modifications via strategic structural element doping[cite: 1]

Advanced Crystallography & Microstructural Analysis

Utilizing comprehensive high-resolution X-ray and electron-based characterization workflows to isolate crystallographic textures, identify complex phases, and assess microstructural changes in synthesized frameworks[cite: 1].

  • Powder and Thin-Film X-ray Diffraction (XRD) with structural refinement analysis[cite: 1]
  • Microstructural evaluation mapping via high-resolution SEM and TEM imaging[cite: 1]
  • Correlating bulk thermophysical properties with localized structural data metrics[cite: 1]
